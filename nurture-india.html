Live Site
Lauching an e-commerce platform empowering local artisans.
Nurture India is a grassroots initiative that supports Indian artisans from underrepresented communities.
Without an online presence to access global markets, I designed and developed their e-commerce website. This platform helps them expand their reach, share their stories, and grow their business with a scalable, user-friendly solution.
Project Approach and Execution
I collaborated closely with the founder to design, build, and empower a platform that amplifies artisans' voices and drives sustainable growth.
Understanding the Goal
I worked closely with Somya (Founder) to identify artisans' needs and customer expectations.
Designing the Experience
The website design was clean and simple, with a focus on the artisans' work and stories. I wanted their creativity to be the star.
Building the Platform
I set up the e-commerce site using Shopify, making sure it was easy to navigate, manage orders, and handle payments.
Integrating Data Analytics
I integrated Google Analytics and GTM that provided insights into traffic, popular products, and customer trends.
Mailchimp for Outreach
I configured Mailchimp for personalized outreach to boost engagement and repeat sales and grow the customer base.
Empowering the Team
I trained Somya's team so that they could manage the site, analytics tools, and email campaigns independently.
Letting the Art Speak for Itself
We kept the product pages simple and clean so the artists' beautiful designs really stand out and speak for themselves, making it easy for customers to appreciate the craftsmanship.
Revenue Growth & Global Reach
Significantly increased customer base and order volume while enabling artisan success worldwide.

What I learned
Launching Nurture India's e-commerce platform and seeing how it helped artisans was really rewarding. I enjoyed working closely with the founder, coming up with ways to share the artisans' stories, and figuring out how to balance technical issues with what users needed.

Looking back, I wish I had done more user testing directly with the artisans. That would have made sure the platform was easier for them to use and manage on their own.
